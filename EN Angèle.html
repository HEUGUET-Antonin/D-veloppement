Angèle, the voice of feminism

Angèle Van Laeken, better known under her artist name "Angèle", is a young Belgian singer, born on December 3, 1995. Belgian singer, born on December 3, 1995. Angèle is today a figure of the feminism in its own right throughout Europe.

Who is Angèle?

His youth

Angèle was born in Uccle in Belgium to a singer father and an actress mother: Serge Van Laeken known as "Marka" and Laurence Bibot. She is also the sister of the rapper Roméo Elvis. The young girl grew up in the Flemish of Linkebeek. Encouraged by her parents, she quickly entered the world of music and began to play the piano.

Her early teenage years were difficult, as she studied in a very strict Catholic school, but she joined the Decroly high school where she was able to develop further thanks to the artistic options offered. but she joined the Decroly high school where she was able to develop further thanks to the artistic options offered.

This school allows her to join a jazz school. At the same time, Angèle started her musical "career" by joining her father's by joining her father's band and giving her first concerts in Brussels cafés.

His career

During 2016, she began to make a name for herself through her covers on Instagram, as well as her cover of Dick Annegarn's "Bruxelles".

In 2017, her rise to fame began, as Angèle opened for the group "Ibeyi" and the Belgian rapper Damso. At the end of the year, the singer released her first song "La loi de Murphy", which received around 30 million views on on YouTube.

She released her second single during 2018 "Je veux tes yeux". The single now has about twenty million views. Afterwards, Angèle played her first concert, festivales francophones, featuring Damso and released her third single "La Thune". The song has 29 million views on YouTube. Angèle became more and more known by the general public and announced her first album "Brol".

During 2019, the artist founded her own label "Angèle VL Records", and on 8 November the reissue of her album was released. and the album was awarded the title of album of the year at the Victoires de la musique.

Angèle is back after some featuring and concerts. Indeed, her second album "95" was released on December 3, 2021, the artist's birthday.

Angèle, artist and feminist

Angèle has been clearly committed to fighting sexism since the beginning and wants to prove that women have a place in the music world. have their place in the music world.

In fact, in a 2018 interview for Télérama, she said: "In the music world, I feel a very strong sexism". The singer also adds that many people do not understand that a young woman can write, compose and produce her own songs. that a young woman can write, compose and produce her own songs. The artist assumes that she is a feminist and wants to fight against sexism wants to fight against sexism, especially the recurrent problem of reducing women to their physical appearance.

On 14 April 2019 the young woman unveiled the video for "Balance ton quoi", her feminist single from her album. The song is considered a feminist anthem and is played during feminist marches or demonstrations. In this track Angèle highlights the feminist movements "Balance ton porc" and "Me too".

Feminist, but not only!

Angèle is committed to many causes other than feminism. Indeed, the artist fights against cancer, defends the LGBT cause and the ecological cause.

In May 2019, the Belgian singer announced her collaboration with the KickCancer foundation. This association fights against paediatric cancer and organises charity races. Angèle became the foundation's godmother that same year and participated in a race on 29 September 2019. In February 2021, the artist organised a tombola for the association. There are many prizes to be won from her but also from other artists such as Dua Lipa, Pierre Niney and many others
